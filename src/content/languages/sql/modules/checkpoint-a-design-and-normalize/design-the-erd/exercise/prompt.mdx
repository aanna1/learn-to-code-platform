This is the first checkpoint — no new ideas, just everything from Modules 02–04 applied to one real, messy dataset. A registrar has been tracking course enrollments in a single spreadsheet, and it's a normalization horror story:

```
enrollments  (one row per student-in-a-course)
+------------+--------------+-----------------+-----------+---------------+---------+-----------+---------------+-------+
| student_id | student_name | student_email   | course_id | course_title  | dept_id | dept_name | dept_building  | grade |
+------------+--------------+-----------------+-----------+---------------+---------+-----------+---------------+-------+
| 1          | Alice        | alice@uni.edu   | 101       | Databases     | 10      | CS        | Lawson         | A     |
| 1          | Alice        | alice@uni.edu   | 205       | Networks      | 10      | CS        | Lawson         | B     |
| 2          | Bao          | bao@uni.edu     | 101       | Databases     | 10      | CS        | Lawson         | A     |
| 3          | Cira         | cira@uni.edu    | 340       | Ethics        | 20      | Phil      | Heavilon       | A     |
+------------+--------------+-----------------+-----------+---------------+---------+-----------+---------------+-------+
```

Every design problem you've studied is in here: Alice's name and email repeat on every course she takes, the CS department's building repeats on every CS course, and there's nowhere to record a course nobody has enrolled in yet. Your job across this two-part checkpoint is to fix it properly — first design (this part), then build (Part 2).

## Part 1 — the design

Two tasks, both graded:

1. **List the functional dependencies.** Read off what determines what. Remember a composite left side (`student_id, course_id -> grade`) and that a right side can list several columns.
2. **Draw the ERD.** Model the four entities and the three relationships. Model each entity with just its **primary key** — represent the foreign keys through the relationships, not as extra attributes — and resolve the student–course many-to-many with the associative entity `enrollment` (composite key `(student_id, course_id)`).

Sketch it in the **draft** box first if it helps (ungraded), then fill in the fields and press **Submit answer**. In Part 2 you'll turn this exact design into real tables.

<Callout type="note">
The ERD is graded on **structure** — entities and their keys, plus each relationship's endpoints, cardinality, and whether it's identifying — never on layout. Endpoints compare both ways, so `student 1:N enrollment` and `enrollment N:1 student` count the same.
</Callout>
