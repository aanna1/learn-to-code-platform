Programs get a lot more fun when they **talk back**. The `input()` function pauses your program, waits for the person to type something and press Enter, and hands back whatever they typed — always as a string.

```python
name = input("What's your name? ")
print("You typed:", name)
```

When you press **Run**, the terminal on the right becomes interactive: the prompt appears, and you type your answer right there.
